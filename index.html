Rules

The game is played in an 8 by 8 board, with the middle 4 cells already containing pieces when the game begins. The top left and bottom right corners have white pieces with the top right and bottom left corners being black. In Othello, the Black player always moves first.


There can only be one piece placed per turn, and a piece can only be placed in an empty cell adjacent to a piece of the opposite color, such that a row of 1 or more of the opponent's discs is flanked by the new disc and an existing disc of the player's color. All of the opponent's discs between these two discs are turned over to match the player's color.


If the current player cannot make a valid move, the turn is passed to the other player. The game is over when neither player can make a move, the board is full, or one of the player concedes the victory to the opponent. In the first two cases, the player with the most pieces of their color on the board wins.
